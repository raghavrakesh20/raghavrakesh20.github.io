Biography

Rakesh Kumar holds a Ph.D. in Ethnomusicology (April 2025) from the International Library of African Music, Rhodes University, South Africa. His thesis, titled "Liminality and States of Being in the Contemporary Music Performances of Goma-Dhamal among Afro-Indian (Siddi) Performers in Gujarat, India," explores the intersection of performance, social identity, and spirituality.

He was an Erasmus Plus Fellow at NOVA University of Lisbon (2023) and holds an M.A. in Ethnomusicology from the Irish World Academy of Music, University of Limerick (2020). Additionally, he earned a Master of Music (M.Mus.) and a Bachelor of Performing Arts (BPA) specializing in Indian Classical Vocal Music from the University of Rajasthan, India.

Most recently, he completed an Advanced Certificate in AI and Machine Learning at the Indian Institute of Skill Development Training (IIDT) in August 2025.

About Me

I am an Ethnomusicologist with a strong background in Indian Classical Vocal Music and a growing interest in the intersection of AI and musicology. My research focuses on the Siddi community in India, exploring themes of liminality, diaspora, and performance.
